Workout Splits

Full Body Split

This is generally the best place for beginners to start. Hitting your whole body several times per week with compound movements that work multiple muscles at a time can be both taxing and strengthening.

3-day split example:

  • Day 1: full body routine
  • Day 2: rest
  • Day 3: full body routine
  • Day 4: rest
  • Day 5: full body routine
  • Day 6/7: rest

Upper/Lower Split

This is a good option for someone who likes the simplicity of the full-body split but wants to train more often at an intermediate level.

4-day split example:

  • Day 1: upper body routine
  • Day 2: lower body routine
  • Day 3: rest
  • Day 4: upper body routine
  • Day 5: lower body routine
  • Day 6/7: rest

Push/Pull/Legs Split

This is a slightly more intermediate to advanced option. The split is great for someone who wants to train six days a week and still have enough time for their muscles to recover between sessions.

6-day split example:

  • Day 1: push - chest, shoulders, triceps
  • Day 2: pull - back, biceps
  • Day 3: legs - quads, glutes, hamstrings, calves
  • Day 4: push - chest, shoulders, triceps
  • Day 5: pull - back, biceps
  • Day 6: legs - quads, glutes, hamstrings, calves
  • Day 7: rest
